Nagpur is dotted with several places that form perfect venues for tourists to indulge in a host of activities, such as picnicking, shopping, clubbing, etc. A few must-try things to do in Nagpur include a visit to the colonial-era landmark – Zero Mile Marker and the popular Raman Science Centre. For leisure, you can explore its popular lakes or adventure parks, while for seeking solace, you can spend time at its revered religious attractions like Shree Ganesh Mandir Tekdi and Deekshabhoomi.
Shopaholics and party-goers too don’t run out of options in the City of Oranges. With an impressive list of things to do, Nagpur vies with other cities in the state to emerge as a popular holiday destination. Here are some of the things that you should not miss if you are in the winter capital of Maharashtra.


1. Do Not Miss the Zero Mile Marker

When in Nagpur, you must not miss a visit to the Zero Mile Marker, which was considered to be the geographic centre point of the country by the British. A tall sandstone pillar was constructed at this particular point by the British that has stood the test of time. This milestone is surrounded by four horse-statues, representing the four cardinal directions. Distances to all major cities were measured using this landmark as a point of reference, and you can still see some of this information engraved on the pillar.


2. Pay Obeisance at Its Religious Sites

If you are here on a spiritual holiday, the city will never disappoint you as it is home to numerous religious sites. Shree Ganesh Mandir Tekdi, Sai Baba Temple, Shri Swaminarayan Temple and Telankhedi Hanuman Mandir are thronged by devotees every year.
Deekshabhoomi that boasts of the world’s largest hollow stupa, Hazrat Tajuddin Baba Dargah of the revered sage, All Saints’ Cathedral with an impressive Gothic-style architecture and Dragon Palace Temple which symbolises Indo-Japanese friendship are other prominent religious sites that attract visitors of all communities. The peaceful ambience of all these places helps nurture your mind, body and soul.


3. De-stress at Amusement and Water Parks

Amusement and water parks are perfect places to spend some fun time with loved ones, and Nagpur offers you some amazing options in this regard. You can plan a visit to Fun N Food Village any time as this amusement park with more than 20 rides and attractions is open 364 days a year, except Diwali. Highland Park, Dwarka Water Park and WaghVille are few other options that you can consider while planning.


4. Appreciate Nature

If you are one of those who love spending time in the lap of nature or appreciating impressive creations, you will surely have a pleasant stay in Nagpur. You can visit Futala Lake that is famous for its halogen-lit fountains, Seminary Hills that offers stunning views of the surroundings and Maharajbagh Zoo that is inhabited by lions, monkeys and several other exquisite animals.
The city is also home to a number of beautifully landscaped gardens that are adorned with aromatic plants and flowers, including the renowned Lata Mangeshkar Musical Garden and Japanese Rose Garden. You can even head to some of the stunning lake gardens, such as Sakkardara Lake Garden and Ambazari Lake Garden, for picnicking and spending quality time with loved ones.


5. Satiate Your Taste Buds

From humble eateries to posh restaurants and cafes, the gastronomical scene in Nagpur is quite engaging, and you can get probably everything that you are craving for – street-food, local specialities, global delicacies, legendary multicuisine dishes, etc.
Depending upon your preference, you can visit fine dining restaurants like Ashoka Restaurant and The Creative Kitchen or head to pocket-friendly eateries like Naivedhyam and Barbecue Restaurant. There are also a number of cafes and bistros to add more fun to your gastronomical journey, such as Mustard Restro Lounge and Bouffage Café & Bistro. So if you are a foodie, you are surely in for a real treat as the ‘City of Oranges’ serves much more than just the citrus fruit!
