Nagpur Metro Route Map Guide



Nagpur Metro is a modern mass transit system serving the city of Nagpur. Still in its initial stages, the system currently has 11 stations across a route length of 13.5 km. Nagpur Metro is being built in two phases, with each phase containing a solitary line. The system has three-coach trains, running at an average speed of 33 km/h and a top speed of 90 km/h. Nagpur Metro uses standard gauge (1435 mm) tracks and 25 kV alternating current supplied by overhead wires.


Nagpur Metro Orange Line
Orange Line, also known as Line 1, is the only currently operational line of Nagpur Metro. Its first section, around 13.5 km in length, was inaugurated on March 8th, 2019. The line currently has 11 stations, whereas 20 have been planned in total. Line 1 runs in a north–south direction, presently connecting the central part of the city with the southern.


Further Extension of Orange Line
The Orange Line is being extended in both directions, to a total of 20 stations and around 23 km route length. From Khapri, it will go further southwards till Metro City, while from Sitabuldi, it will move northwards to Automotive Square.


Nagpur Metro Lesser-Known Facts
Train depot house residential complexes
India’s greenest metro system with biodigesters designed by Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)
Every station has a different design theme
Multimodal passes for metro and city buses
Metro to be extended to the satellite towns of Hingana, Kanhan and Buttibori
